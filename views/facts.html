Facts About Professor Michael Cooley

FACT 1:

Michael Cooley was an Irish-born engineer known for his work on the social effects of technology, particularly "Socially Useful Production" and "Human-Centered Systems."

FACT 2:

He played a significant role in workplace activism at Lucas Aerospace in the late 1970s, where he was involved in creating The Lucas Plan, a proposal to convert military production to socially useful products.

FACT 3:

Cooley was awarded the Right Livelihood Award in 1981 for designing and promoting the theory and practice of human-centered, socially useful production.

FACT 4:

Michael Cooley's innovative thinking on technology led to his concept of "skill-centred systems," which aim to prioritize the enhancement and utilization of human skills within technological environments.

FACT 5:

His work extended into the political arena, where he was a key figure in the '70s advocating for socially useful and sustainable production models in industry.

FACT 6:

Cooley's contributions to technology and society were recognized internationally, exemplified by his receipt of the Alternative Nobel Prize, the Right Livelihood Award.
